## Session Log

### 01-Campaign/01-Into-the-Mists-and-Death-House.md

**Overall Player Score:** 8 / 10

**Player-Fun Highlights (What feels great to play):**
- The mist-trap intro is vivid and creepy, with strong sensory details that make Barovia feel oppressive from the start.
- Death House is presented as a full cinematic experience, with rich narration, clear beats, and lots of prompts that invite player decisions.
- Frequent Strahd-adjacent vibes (ravens, silhouettes, unseen watchers) keep tension high without forcing a direct confrontation too early.
- The script-style NPC dialogue and acting notes will help a shy or awkward DM deliver scenes that *sound* cool at the table.

**Potential Player Pain Points (What might drag or frustrate):**
- Some sections are very text-heavy and could result in long DM monologues. Players may get restless if narration chunks run too long without a choice or check.
- A few decision points are implied rather than explicitly framed as choices. New players thrive when the DM clearly says things like: "You can investigate X, follow Y, or do something entirely different. What do you do?"
- Death House is deadly by design; a gentle reminder in the text about telegraphing danger and offering non-lethal outs (especially for brand-new groups) would protect player enjoyment.

**Player-Facing Suggestions for Improvement:**
- Add more short breaks in the narration with explicit questions or micro-prompts such as:
  - "As the house looms over you, what detail about it unsettles your character the most?" (Prompt for character expression.)
  - "Who steps inside first, and why?" (Prompt for intra-party dynamics.)
- Where Strahd’s presence is implied, add 1–2 optional, very short visions or whispers the DM can use *if the table seems excited* about spooky interaction, such as:
  - A cold voice in one character’s mind: *"You are not the first… but you may be the last."*
- Include 1–2 explicit safety-valve suggestions for the Death House finale (e.g., ways the house might injure-but-spare a character, or how a collapse might separate but not kill them) to keep the tone tense but not miserable for new players.

**Fun Factor Summary:**
This intro and Death House read as highly engaging and cinematic for players, with lots of atmosphere and a strong sense of mystery and danger. With a few more clearly labeled decision points and some built-in safety levers for the most lethal moments, it will be an excellent opening experience for a brand new group.
